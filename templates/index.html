WELCOME TO MyDawa Medical Clinic

Our mission at MyDawa Medical Clinic Hospital is to provide compassionate, high-quality healthcare services to all individuals in our community. We are dedicated to improving the health and well-being of our patients through innovation, collaboration, and excellence in patient care.

Why Choose MyDawa Medical Clinic?

MyDawa Medical Clinic, located in a vibrant city, is dedicated to providing compassionate, high-quality healthcare services to its community. With 24/7 emergency care, comprehensive inpatient and outpatient services, specialized clinics, and advanced technology, the hospital focuses on patient well-being and community outreach. Guided by values of integrity, compassion, and excellence, MyDawa Medical Clinic strives to deliver exceptional care and promote health education and access for all.

Frequently Asked Questions

These are questions you may want to ask

FAQ: How do I schedule or cancel a follow-up appointment with a specialist?

To schedule or cancel a follow-up appointment with a specialist, you can contact the hospital's scheduling department or the specialist's office directly. Be sure to have your appointment details handy, including the date and time, as well as any reference numbers or instructions provided during your initial consultation.

FAQ: What should I do if I need medical attention outside of regular office hours?

If you require medical attention outside of regular office hours, you can visit the hospital's emergency department (ED). The ED is open 24/7 and is equipped to handle a wide range of medical emergencies, from minor injuries to life-threatening conditions.

FAQ: How can I request my medical records?

To request your medical records, you can contact the hospital's medical records department. They will provide you with the necessary forms to fill out and guide you through the process. Depending on the hospital's policy, you may need to provide identification and sign a release form.

FAQ: Can I bring personal belongings for a hospitalized loved one?

Yes, you can bring personal belongings for a hospitalized loved one, such as clothing, toiletries, and comfort items. It's advisable to check with the hospital regarding any restrictions on specific items, such as electronics or valuables. Additionally, labeling belongings with the patient's name can help prevent loss or confusion.

FAQ: What should I expect upon discharge from the hospital?

Upon discharge from the hospital, you can expect to receive instructions from your healthcare provider regarding post-discharge care, medications, and follow-up appointments. You may also receive written discharge instructions and prescriptions. It's essential to review and follow these instructions carefully to ensure a smooth transition back to home or ongoing care. If you have any questions or concerns, don't hesitate to ask your healthcare team for clarification.
